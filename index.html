Built specifically for subreddit moderators who want data, not guesswork.

📊
Engagement Dashboard
Visualize post volume, comment depth, and upvote ratios over time. Spot trends before they become problems.
🚨
Anomaly Alerts
Get notified when post volume spikes, new account concentration rises, or engagement drops below baseline.
🔑
Keyword Tracker
See which topics and keywords dominate your community week-over-week. Identify emerging narratives early.

Compare health metrics across communities side by side. Perfect for mods managing multiple subreddits.
// How it works

Simple setup,
powerful insights

ModPulse is open-source and self-hostable. Get started in minutes.

Connect your subreddit
Authenticate with Reddit via OAuth. ModPulse only requests read permissions — it never posts, votes, or takes any action.
Configure your thresholds
Set your alert thresholds for post volume spikes, new account ratios, and engagement drops based on your community's norms.
Monitor your dashboard
Watch real-time metrics update as your community grows. Get alerts the moment something unusual happens.
Receive weekly digests
Get a concise weekly summary delivered to your inbox. Spend less time moderating, more time building community.
// Privacy & Policy

Built with
Responsible
data use

ModPulse is read-only by design. We believe moderation tools should empower communities, not exploit them.

[✓]
Read-only access — ModPulse never posts, comments, votes, or takes any automated action on Reddit.
[✓]
Public data only — Only accesses posts and comments already visible to any user.
[✓]
No data resale — Community data is never sold, shared, or monetized.
[✓]
Rate limit compliant — Strictly respects Reddit's API rate limits (60 req/min).
[✓]
Opt-in only — Moderators must explicitly add their subreddit. No community is analyzed without consent.
